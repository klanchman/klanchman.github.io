Who I Am

Software Developer...

I'm a software developer, and I love what I do. I enjoy working on all sorts of projects, especially mobile and full-stack web application development. People interact with all kinds of software every day. It's my goal to ensure when they use anything I wrote, they have the best experience possible.

Whenever I find some spare time and I have an urge to create something, I like to work on personal side projects.

Sometimes it's just something small, like using my Raspberry Pi to automate my lights and camera based on my location and the time of day.

Other times it's a full-blown application. I worked on an iOS app for Reddit for a while. I got pretty far along, but between more apps being released and Reddit changing how to access their API, I decided to stop.

I also have an iOS app for AniList. It's called Ryuusei, and it's available on the App Store! There probably isn't a huge market for this app, but I'm doing it more for fun and my own use than anything.

...And a Bit More

Besides software development, I'm interested in learning more about user interface and graphic design. I have a deep appreciation for applications that have a clean and functional interface. Whenever I am working on the UI for a project, my goal is to make it something that I would enjoy using myself.

I also have an interest in system administration and DevOps. I've managed systems in an office environment, I love my ever-growing homelab, and I like setting up CI/CD for work projects and side projects.

Tech Enthusiast

I love just about anything related to technology. I'm always reading about new technologies, languages, frameworks, and tools. It's so satisfying to find new tech to learn about and try out.

Warranty Voider

I love to experiment with my devices to see what I can do with them. I've voided the warranty on nearly every device I've owned, usually through software modifications.

It started with an MP3 player: I flashed a customizable 3rd party firmware on it. Later I started using Android phones. I rooted and flashed custom ROMs on every one of them. Then I got an iPhone, so I just had to jailbreak it.

Lately I haven't been voiding warranties though, instead hacking away on a Raspberry Pi or in a Proxmox VM.

Writer and Photographer?

Well, sort of. I enjoy things other than tech, too. (I swear!)

I really like writing; I keep a small blog that I update occasionally when I think of something I want to write about. Admittedly, those updates are very infrequent.

I also enjoy photography, though I'm not particularly good at it. I try to bring my camera whenever I know I could see something interesting, but usually I'll make do with my phone's camera.
